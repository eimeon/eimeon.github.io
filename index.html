WEEK 2 - IDEATION & PROTOTYPING

During the second week, I pushed further the idea of the previous week to create an outfit for a festival. I spend time sketching different ideas. I was really inspired by the butterfly which represents a colorful but peaceful beauty of nature. In addition, I thought about the modulable part of the design and printed the first prototype of a module.

STEP 1

The first step consisted in a long and fastidious sketching phase where I really putted all my creativity to create the final design. After numerous sketches, I settled for the design the can be seen in the picture.

PANELS

The top is composed of 8 different and modulable panels shaped like small drops

STEP 2

The next step was about finding an idea of how the texture of the design should be like. I would like the texture to be small flowers with eventually butterfly on tops. However there is one constraint. As this design is made to be wearable, the small modules can not have sharp angles in direct contact of the skin.

MODULES

With the constraint of having no sharp angles in mind, I thought of multiple options. I wanted to keep the idea of tiny flowers intermodulable but binding them together with no extra element seemed impossible. However, it was possible to bind them with disks. The idea is that each disk will bind three modules together.

STEP 3

Step 3 was about creating a viable 3D-design for the modular part. For this part, I used the software Shapr3D, which is an intuitive 3D modelisation software made to make the design of 3D object for printing easier. I created this snowflake like shape based on the previous sketch of the modular element, as well as a disks with a small slot to try inserting the snowflake in. Here the height of the snowflake is 0.50mm to assure that it stays as bendable as possible.

3D PRINT

Here is a picture of the actual 3D print of the module. I printed with 2 layers of 0.30mm to save time even thought my model was 0.50mm. For that reason, the print of the disk, and more precisely the slot of the disk did not succeed. For next week, I want to test different heights for the snowflake and the disk and different slicing.
